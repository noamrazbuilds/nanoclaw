# PKA & NanoClaw Use Cases

A reference document capturing 20 real-world use cases for the Personal Knowledge Assistant (PKA) and NanoClaw systems. Use this as context when designing features, agent behaviors, and workflows.

---

## Part 1: PKA in Action
*Ten moments where a Personal Knowledge Assistant changes everything.*

### 1. The Meeting Save
You have five minutes before a client meeting and your mind is blank. Ask your PKA what you know about this person and it surfaces every note, conversation, and detail from years of contact. You walk in knowing their daughter just started college — and they light up that you remembered.

**What PKA does:** Surfaces relationship history, past conversations, personal details, and context on demand.

---

### 2. The Book Club Rescue
Book club is in an hour and you barely remember the plot. Your PKA has your highlighted passages, margin notes, and reactions from the week you read it. You walk in with real opinions, specific pages, and one quote that stops the room.

**What PKA does:** Stores and retrieves reading annotations, highlights, and personal reactions to books.

---

### 3. Prepared Patient
"When did this start?" is the hardest question at a doctor's appointment. Your PKA has been quietly logging every symptom you ever mentioned — in your own words, with dates. Hand your doctor an actual timeline instead of a shrug.

**What PKA does:** Maintains a longitudinal health log from casual notes, surfaces patterns and timelines on request.

---

### 4. The Birthday Oracle
Your dad's birthday is today. PKA surfaces what you got him last year, what he mentioned wanting in passing three months ago, and what came up during your last visit. The perfect gift was already in your notes — waiting to be found.

**What PKA does:** Captures offhand mentions from notes and conversations, links them to people and dates.

---

### 5. The Project Homecoming
Back from two weeks off, staring at a codebase with no memory of where you left off. PKA has your last session notes: what you decided, what you deferred, what was confusing, and exactly where you were headed. You're productive within minutes.

**What PKA does:** Preserves working context, decisions-in-progress, and open questions for projects.

---

### 6. The Gift Oracle
Six months ago your partner mentioned in passing that they'd always wanted to try pottery. You didn't write it down. But you were talking near your phone, and your PKA logged it. The perfect gift was sitting in your memory the whole time.

**What PKA does:** Passively captures wishes, preferences, and desires mentioned in passing; links to people.

---

### 7. The Recipe Time Machine
That pasta you made two years ago that everyone raved about — you never wrote down the recipe properly. But you fired off a quick note right after, and PKA finds it in seconds. Some things are worth remembering exactly.

**What PKA does:** Full-text search across years of notes; surfaces content regardless of how it was originally filed.

---

### 8. The Research Thread
Deep in new research, you feel a familiar pull — like you've been here before. PKA surfaces a note from two years ago on a related topic you'd completely forgotten. Suddenly the new and old connect, and the whole picture snaps into focus.

**What PKA does:** Semantic search and connection-surfacing across the full knowledge base, across time.

---

### 9. The Decision Mirror
Big decision, no clarity. PKA surfaces your own past writing: your values, your reflections on similar crossroads, what you regretted and what you didn't. Your past self already did the thinking. You just needed to hear yourself again.

**What PKA does:** Retrieves personal values, past decisions, and reflective writing to support current decisions.

---

### 10. The Year in Review
"What did I even do this year?" Your PKA has the whole picture: books read, skills built, problems solved, moments captured. The year turns out to be richer than you remembered — and you have the receipts to prove it.

**What PKA does:** Aggregates and synthesizes activity across the full knowledge base into a coherent narrative.

---

## Part 2: Better Together
*Ten moments where PKA and NanoClaw work in concert — and why you need both.*

### 11. Good Morning
Every morning NanoClaw reads your PKA and sends a WhatsApp briefing: what needs follow-up, what you were in the middle of, what's on today. Two systems, one signal. NanoClaw is your daily pilot; PKA is the flight log.

**NanoClaw does:** Scheduled task reads PKA, synthesizes, delivers via WhatsApp.
**PKA does:** Holds the full context — notes, calendar, open threads — that the briefing is drawn from.

---

### 12. Capture Anything
Dinner conversation, a great restaurant tip, a name to remember. Text it to NanoClaw on WhatsApp. It saves to PKA. No friction, no forgetting. NanoClaw is the input layer — always in your pocket. PKA is the memory — always at your side.

**NanoClaw does:** Receives casual capture via WhatsApp, routes to PKA vault.
**PKA does:** Stores, indexes, and makes the capture searchable and connectable later.

---

### 13. Send and Forget
Drop a PDF into WhatsApp. NanoClaw files it to your PKA vault. Three months later, ask about the payment terms and get the exact clause back. NanoClaw handles the inbox; PKA handles the archive. Together: nothing slips through.

**NanoClaw does:** Receives file attachments via WhatsApp, saves to PKA attachments directory.
**PKA does:** Indexes document content, enables full-text retrieval months later.

---

### 14. Your Research Team
Ask NanoClaw to research something before Thursday's meeting. It searches, reads, synthesizes, and stores the findings in PKA. You get a morning briefing with everything you need. NanoClaw does the legwork; PKA holds the result.

**NanoClaw does:** Agentic web research, synthesis, filing to PKA vault.
**PKA does:** Stores research output, connects it to related existing notes.

---

### 15. Who Is This Person?
Unfamiliar number calling. Quick text to NanoClaw: "Who is Marcus Chen?" It queries PKA in seconds: when you met, what you talked about, what you promised to follow up on. You answer the call ready, and Marcus is delighted you remembered.

**NanoClaw does:** Real-time query interface via WhatsApp; queries PKA on demand.
**PKA does:** Holds contact history, interaction notes, and follow-up commitments.

---

### 16. Sunday Digest
Every Sunday morning, NanoClaw pulls a digest from PKA: what came in this week, what connects to ongoing projects, what needs attention next. Two minutes of reading, one week of clarity. The week begins intentionally instead of reactively.

**NanoClaw does:** Scheduled weekly synthesis, delivered via WhatsApp.
**PKA does:** Accumulates the week's captures, notes, and open threads that the digest draws from.

---

### 17. The Smart Nudge
Six months ago you noted "follow up with Emma in spring." Spring arrived. PKA noticed. NanoClaw sent you a message. The follow-up happened. Neither system forgot — and neither did you, because you didn't have to.

**NanoClaw does:** Monitors PKA for time-sensitive notes; sends proactive reminders via WhatsApp.
**PKA does:** Stores the original intent with enough context for NanoClaw to act on it correctly.

---

### 18. The 2AM Idea
Voice message NanoClaw at 2am with a half-formed idea. It transcribes, tags, and stores it in PKA with context. Morning-you finds it intact — with related notes already surfaced. No idea lost to sleep. No friction between thought and memory.

**NanoClaw does:** Voice transcription, tagging, routing to PKA; surfaces related notes proactively.
**PKA does:** Stores the idea, connects it to the existing knowledge graph.

---

### 19. The Symptom Detective
Log symptoms casually through NanoClaw over weeks — just texting as you would a friend. Before your appointment, ask for a compiled health timeline. PKA built it from every message. Your doctor says it's the most useful patient history they've seen.

**NanoClaw does:** Conversational health logging; compiles and formats on request.
**PKA does:** Maintains the longitudinal symptom log; enables timeline synthesis.

---

### 20. Connecting the Dots
Notes from 2023. A book from 2024. A goal you set in 2025. NanoClaw's consolidation agent sees the thread running through your PKA and sends you the insight: your past selves were working toward the same thing all along. The dots were always there. Now they're connected.

**NanoClaw does:** Consolidation agent periodically scans PKA for latent connections; surfaces them proactively.
**PKA does:** Holds the full corpus of notes across years that makes pattern detection possible.

---

## Summary: Division of Labor

| Capability | PKA | NanoClaw |
|---|---|---|
| Long-term memory | ✓ stores everything | — |
| Mobile capture | — | ✓ WhatsApp input |
| Scheduled delivery | — | ✓ morning briefings, digests |
| Semantic search | ✓ local embeddings | — |
| Agentic research | — | ✓ web search + synthesis |
| Proactive nudges | — | ✓ monitors + messages |
| File handling | ✓ vault + indexing | ✓ receives + routes |
| Pattern detection | ✓ full corpus | ✓ consolidation agent |
| Real-time queries | ✓ answers | ✓ interface |

**The core insight:** PKA is the brain. NanoClaw is the nervous system. Neither is complete without the other.
